# Controls and Map

![Main map and HUD](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/arn-c0de/ZombieEscape-Preview/main/images/main.png)

## Movement modes

ZombieEscape supports two movement modes:

- **GPS mode** follows your device's real-world position.
- **Manual mode** lets you move with the on-screen joystick.

Use the movement-mode button above the joystick to switch modes. Switching back to GPS teleports the character from the manual position to the current real-world GPS location, so the game asks for confirmation and shows the approximate distance first.

If GPS is weak, wait for a fresh fix or move outdoors. Location permission must be enabled for GPS mode.

## Walking and running

- Drag the joystick in the direction you want to move.
- Use the running control while the joystick is held to run; releasing it returns to walking.
- Movement consumes stamina. Slow down or use **Rest** when stamina is low.
- The joystick can be placed on the left or right in **Settings**. Nearby action controls move to the opposite side.

## Map interaction

- Tap markers to inspect zombies, NPCs, vehicles, events, or other points of interest.
- Long-press a building to open its building menu. Depending on the building, you can loot/search, claim it, manage an owned house, or view information.
- Pan and zoom the map normally with touch gestures.
- The layer/map button controls map presentation. Satellite imagery may also be available from the Admin tools in development builds.

## Main map buttons

The large colored buttons provide fast access to common functions. Depending on the current state, they open movement, menu, inventory, quick actions, interactions, or map-layer controls. Vehicle-specific controls replace or supplement them while driving.

## Actions page

![Actions page](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/arn-c0de/ZombieEscape-Preview/main/images/actions.png)

The **Actions** page offers:

- **Enter Building**
- **Search Area**
- **Loot Items**
- **Talk to NPC**
- **Access Vehicle**
- **Set Home Location**

Actions depend on distance and context. If nothing happens, move closer and try again.

## Quick Actions

The lightning button lists only useful actions for the current surroundings, grouped into Loot & Collect, Combat, Survival, Crafting, Transport, Base, and Social. Examples include collecting ground items, looting nearby buildings, eating and drinking, resting, opening medical care, cooking, accessing vehicles, storage, and trading.
